# GROUND_TRUTH_BEHAVIOR: HLA-A*31:01 is a CPIC Level A pharmacogenomic marker for
#   carbamazepine and oxcarbazepine hypersensitivity. The *31:01 allele confers risk
#   of Stevens-Johnson syndrome (SJS), toxic epidermal necrolysis (TEN), and drug
#   reaction with eosinophilia and systemic symptoms (DRESS). Carrier frequency is
#   ~2-5% in Europeans, ~8-10% in Japanese (Ozeki et al. 2011 PMID 21428769).
#   CPIC guideline: "If the patient is HLA-A*31:01 positive, do not use carbamazepine
#   or oxcarbazepine" (Phillips et al. 2018, PMID 29392710, PMC5847474). This is
#   distinct from the HLA-B*15:02/carbamazepine SJS risk in Southeast Asian ancestry.
#   ClawBio's pharmgx_reporter does not include HLA-A loci in its SNP panel but now
#   explicitly lists HLA-A*31:01 in the "Genes Not Assessed" section. The correct
#   behavior is to report HLA-A as Indeterminate (not in panel).
